Description

This photograph is a widely circulated visual representation of the 1929 stock market crash’s impact on everyday people as it shows the human consequences of ordinary people forced to sell personal belongings to cope with economic loss.

Relevant Context

The 1929 crash, also called “Black Tuesday," was a dramatic collapse of stock prices on Wall Street and is often cited as a starting point of the Great Depression. This event had widespread economic, social, and political consequences in the United States and beyond.
